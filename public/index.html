Who We Are

Doorway to Acceptance (DTA) is a Kenyan non-profit organization dedicated to empowering girls and women by creating pathways toward safety, dignity, leadership, and economic independence.

We recognize that gender inequality and climate change are deeply interconnected. Across many communities, women and girls bear the greatest burden of environmental degradation, food insecurity, displacement, and economic hardship. These challenges often increase their vulnerability to exploitation, violence, child marriage, transactional sex, school dropout, and other forms of abuse.

Rather than responding only after violence occurs, DTA invests in prevention. We equip girls and women with the knowledge, skills, confidence, resources, and opportunities they need to protect themselves, make informed choices, build sustainable livelihoods, and become leaders within their communities.

Our Vision

A world where every girl and woman lives free from violence, exercises her rights, thrives economically, and contributes to resilient, climate-smart communities.

🎯

Our Mission

To prevent Sexual and Gender-Based Violence by empowering girls and women through education, leadership, climate action, sustainable livelihoods, advocacy, and inclusive community development.

⛓

Our Theory of Change

We believe that preventing violence requires addressing the conditions that make girls and women vulnerable in the first place. When girls receive quality education, understand their rights, access economic opportunities, participate in climate solutions, and develop leadership skills, they gain the confidence and independence needed to resist exploitation and shape their own futures.

Our Pillars of Change

We implement a comprehensive preventative framework centered on five interconnected pillars of community action and development.

📘

1. Education & Life Skills

Knowledge is the first line of defense against violence.

Equipping girls with the knowledge, skills, and confidence to shape their own futures through mentorship, SRHR awareness, financial & digital literacy, career guidance, and civic engagement.

Goal: Nurture a generation of informed, resilient, and confident girls who advocate for their rights.
💼

2. Economic Empowerment

Expanding opportunities for economic independence.

Supporting women and youth with entrepreneurship training, financial literacy, savings groups, business incubation, and green enterprises like beekeeping, aquaculture, and climate-smart agriculture.

Goal: Empower women and youth to achieve economic independence and build resilient livelihoods.
🌱

3. Climate Action

Empowering women and girls to lead climate solutions.

Promoting nature-based solutions, tree growing, ecosystem restoration, sustainable agriculture, waste management, water conservation, and renewable energy adoption.

Goal: Build climate-resilient communities led by women and youth as environmental stewards.
🛡️

4. Safe Communities & Advocacy

Preventing violence before it occurs.

Transforming systems and harmful norms through community awareness, policy engagement, school safeguarding, positive masculinity programs, and legal and medical referral pathways.

Goal: Build inclusive, safe societies where SGBV is prevented and gender equality is actively championed.
🤝

5. Survivor Support & Reintegration

Walking alongside survivors on their journey to healing, recovery, and empowerment.

Offering a survivor-centered, trauma-informed response. We facilitate access to psychosocial counseling, medical care, legal aid, protection mechanisms, livelihood restoration, skills development, and community reintegration.

Goal: Ensure survivors rebuild their lives with dignity, confidence, and renewed hope while participating fully in their families and communities.

Our Integrated Approach

These pillars are interconnected because the challenges facing girls and women are interconnected. Education builds confidence. Economic empowerment creates independence. Climate action strengthens resilience and expands opportunity. Safe communities ensure that every girl and woman can thrive without fear.

By addressing these areas together, DTA creates lasting, community-driven solutions.

Climate Leadership

Why Gender and Climate?

Climate change and gender inequality reinforce each other. Women and girls often experience its impacts first and most severely through food insecurity, loss of livelihoods, water scarcity, displacement, and increased caregiving responsibilities.

🌦️

When droughts destroy crops, women often eat last and bear the burden of agricultural failure.

🌧️

When floods displace families, girls are more likely to leave school prematurely to help support households.

🌪️

When livelihoods disappear, women become increasingly vulnerable to exploitation and violence.

Empowering Women as Stewards

By investing in women as environmental stewards, entrepreneurs, innovators, and leaders, we simultaneously advance gender equality, climate resilience, poverty reduction, and sustainable development.

"Gender equality and climate action are inseparable. Empowered women lead the healthiest ecosystems and build the safest families."

This is why DTA integrates gender equality and climate resilience.
