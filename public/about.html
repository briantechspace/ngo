Our Story & Purpose

Learn how Doorway to Acceptance (DTA) creates safety, dignity, and economic resilience for girls and women across Kenya.

Investing in SGBV Prevention

Across many Kenyan communities, gender inequality and climate change are deeply interconnected. Women and girls bear the greatest burden of environmental degradation, food insecurity, water scarcity, displacement, and economic hardship.

These severe challenges often increase their vulnerability to exploitation, sexual and gender-based violence (SGBV), child marriage, transactional sex, school dropout, and other forms of abuse.

Rather than responding only after violence occurs, **DTA invests in prevention**. We equip girls and women with the knowledge, skills, confidence, resources, and opportunities they need to protect themselves, make informed choices, build sustainable livelihoods, and become leaders within their families and communities.

Our work integrates gender equality, climate resilience, economic empowerment, education, mental wellbeing, and community action to build safer and more inclusive societies.

👁

Our Vision

A world where every girl and woman lives free from violence, exercises her rights, thrives economically, and contributes to resilient, climate-smart communities.

🎯

Our Mission

To prevent Sexual and Gender-Based Violence by empowering girls and women through education, leadership, climate action, sustainable livelihoods, advocacy, and inclusive community development.

⛓

Theory of Change

When girls receive quality education, understand their rights, access economic opportunities, participate in climate solutions, and develop leadership skills, they gain the confidence and independence needed to resist exploitation and shape their own futures.

Women and girls are disproportionately affected by climate disasters due to traditional caregiving roles, limited economic independence, and displacement risks. By training women in green enterprises like beekeeping, regenerative farming, and environmental advocacy, we simultaneously reduce poverty, advance safety, and build climate resilience.
DTA provides a survivor-centered, trauma-informed approach. We work collaboratively with medical, legal, and psychosocial professionals to facilitate referrals. Beyond immediate response, we support long-term recovery by offering skills development, savings groups, and community reintegration.
Donations are securely verified through our backend endpoints linked to Paystack integrations. Ninety percent (90%) of all public donations are allocated directly to procurement of field tools, tree saplings, educational resources, and workshop organization. Only 10% supports administrative checkups.
